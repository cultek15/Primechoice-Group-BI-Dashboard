# Dashboard Pages Overview

Detailed guide to each of the 5 dashboard pages, including purpose, metrics, visuals, and key insights.

---

## Page 1: Executive Scorecard

**Audience:** CEO, Group Managing Director, C-Suite Executives  
**Refresh Frequency:** Daily  
**Use Case:** Strategic performance review, board reporting

### Purpose
High-level overview of business health across key dimensions. Designed for quick executive decision-making.

### KPI Cards (Top Row)

| Metric | Value | Insight |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Total Revenue** | ₦29.42bn | Cumulative 3-year sales performance |
| **Gross Profit** | ₦8.01bn | Profit before operating expenses |
| **Gross Margin %** | 27.23% | Profitability per sale (industry benchmark: 25-30%) |
| **EBITDA %** | 22.04% | Operating efficiency (earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, amortization) |

### Chart 1: Revenue vs Budget (Combo Chart)
- **Type:** Clustered column + line chart
- **X-axis:** Months (Jan-Dec)
- **Columns:** Actual Revenue (blue)
- **Line:** Budget Revenue (green target line)
- **Insight:** Tracks monthly sales performance against budget; budget consistently above actual
- **Action:** Investigate budget realism; either adjust forecast or address sales shortfalls

### Chart 2: Revenue by Product Category (Horizontal Bar Chart)
- **Type:** Ranked horizontal bar chart
- **Shows:** Total revenue by product category
- **Top Categories:**
  - Audio & TV: ~₦5.0bn (highest revenue driver)
  - Phones & Accessories: ~₦3.8bn
  - Small Appliances: ~₦2.3bn
- **Insight:** Electronics categories dominate; clear product mix concentration
- **Action:** Ensure adequate inventory for top categories; consider promotional focus on under-represented categories

### Chart 3: Profit Bridge (Waterfall Chart)
- **Type:** Waterfall visualization
- **Stages:** Revenue → Gross Profit → EBITDA → Opex → COGS → Total
- **Shows:** Where profit is "lost" at each stage
- **Color Coding:**
  - Green: Positive contributions (Revenue, Gross Profit)
  - Blue: Decreases (COGS, Opex)
  - Dark blue: Total (EBITDA)
- **Key Observation:**
  - Revenue: ₦29.42bn (starting point)
  - Gross Profit: ₦8.01bn
  - EBITDA: ~₦6.5bn (after opex)
- **Insight:** Visual representation of profitability chain; COGS and Opex are largest profit drains
- **Action:** Focus on COGS reduction (negotiate supplier prices) and Opex control (efficiency improvements)

### Chart 4: Revenue by Region (Geographic Map)
- **Type:** Geographic heat map of Nigeria
- **Shows:** Total revenue by region
- **Regional Distribution:**
  - SW-NG (South West/Lagos): Blue (darkest - ~42%)
  - SS-NG (South South): Blue (lighter)
  - SE-NG (South East): Gray (lighter)
  - NC-NG (North Central): Gray (lightest)
- **Insight:** Heavy concentration in Southwest region (Lagos effect)
- **Action:** Growth opportunity in underperforming regions; consider targeted expansion

### Chart 5: YoY Revenue Growth Trend (Multi-Line Chart)
- **Type:** Line chart with 3 lines (one per year)
- **X-axis:** Months (Jan-Dec)
- **Lines:**
  - 2022: Black (baseline - ~₦0.8bn/month)
  - 2023: Blue (showing growth)
  - 2024: Dark blue (strongest growth)
- **Pattern:** All years show similar seasonal shape; December spike visible
- **Growth Rate:** 2023 vs 2022: +9% | 2024 vs 2023: +18%
- **Insight:** Strong growth trajectory; repeatable seasonal patterns
- **Action:** Plan inventory and staffing around seasonal peaks; build on momentum

### Slicers
- **State:** Filter all charts to specific region(states) (SW-NG, SS-NG, SE-NG, NC-NG, or All)
- **Year:** Filter to specific year(s) or all

### Key Questions This Page Answers
1. Are we hitting revenue targets?
2. Is profitability improving?
3. Which regions are strongest/weakest?
4. What's driving growth?

## Page 2: Sales & Product Performance

**Audience:** Category Managers, Product Managers, Marketing Team  
**Refresh Frequency:** Weekly  
**Use Case:** Product strategy, pricing decisions, promotion planning

### Purpose
Deep dive into product-level performance. Helps category managers understand margins, market mix, and product health. Focused catalog of 228 active SKUs ensures every product is actively sold.

### KPI Cards (Top Row)

| Metric | Value | Insight |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Total Units Sold** | 5M units | Volume metric; shows how many items moved |
| **Avg Product Margin %** | 27.23% | Average profitability across all products |
| **Products Sold in Period** | 228 SKUs | Focused catalog with 100% SKU utilization (all selling) |

### Chart 1: Sales by Department (Treemap)
- **Type:** Treemap visualization
- **Shows:** Revenue by product department with size-proportional blocks
- **Department Breakdown:**
  - Electronics: ₦11.16bn (largest block)
  - Clothing: ₦7.07bn
  - Household: ₦3.63bn
  - Food: ₦3.60bn
  - Non-Food: ₦2.82bn
- **Insight:** Electronics dominates; clear revenue concentration
- **Action:** Allocate shelf space and marketing budget proportionally

### Chart 2: Product Margin Analysis (Scatter Plot)
- **Type:** Scatter chart with colored bubbles
- **X-axis:** Total Revenue (₦ billions)
- **Y-axis:** Product Margin % (20-40%)
- **Bubble Size:** Units sold (larger = more volume)
- **Legend:** Brand color-coding
- **Quadrants Visible:**
  - **Top-right:** High revenue, high margin (stars) ⭐
  - **Top-left:** Low revenue, high margin (niche/premium)
  - **Bottom-right:** High revenue, low margin (volume commodity)
  - **Bottom-left:** Low revenue, low margin (problem products)
- **Insight:** Mix of strategies visible; some high-volume low-margin products
- **Action:** Consider price increases on profitable products; investigate margin compression on volume items

### Chart 3: Top 5 Products by Revenue (Horizontal Bar Chart)
- **Type:** Ranked horizontal bar chart
- **Top Products:**
  1. Peak Milk Phones: ₦1.35bn
  2. Johnson an Audio: ₦1.10bn
  3. PrimeChoic Audio: ₦0.86bn
  4. Pepsi Audio & TV: ₦0.86bn
  5. Golden Pen Audio: ₦0.82bn
- **Insight:** Top 5 products represent ~₦5.0bn (~25% of category revenue)
- **Action:** Ensure adequate inventory; premium promotions; category leadership

### Chart 4: Top 5 Product Category Trend by Revenue (Stacked Line Chart)
- **Type:** Multi-line stacked area chart
- **X-axis:** Months (Jan-Dec)
- **Lines (Categories):**
  - Audio & TV: Dark blue (top line)
  - Phones & Accessories: Blue
  - Small Appliances: Green
  - Women's Clothing: Teal
  - Others (stacked below)
- **Pattern:**
  - Audio & TV: Highest throughout, slight Dec spike
  - Phones: Steady mid-tier
  - Appliances: Stable low-mid
  - Notable: December shows uplift across most categories
- **Insight:** Category seasonality varies; Electronics stable, Appliances seasonal
- **Action:** Plan category promotions around observed seasonal patterns

### Slicers
- **Category:** Filter to specific product category (All, Audio & TV, Phones, etc.)
- **Region:** Filter to regional sales patterns
- **Year:** Compare year-over-year product performance

### Key Questions This Page Answers
1. Which products are most profitable?
2. Are we selling all 228 products or just a subset?
3. What's the product mix risk?
4. Which categories need attention?

## Page 3: Store Operations & Inventory

**Audience:** Store Managers, Regional Directors, Operations Team  
**Refresh Frequency:** Weekly/Daily for alerts  
**Use Case:** Inventory management, out-of-stock prevention, shrinkage control

### Purpose
Store-level operational health. Critical for managing inventory risks and ensuring product availability.

### KPI Cards (Top Row)

| Metric | Value | Insight |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Total Stores in Network** | 40 | Complete store count across all regions |
| **Avg Days of Cover** | 10.05 days | Average inventory will last ~10 days (RISKY - low safety stock) |
| **Shrinkage %** | 0.94% | Waste rate is excellent (<1% is world-class) |

### Chart 1: Out of Stock Trend (Line Chart)
- **Type:** Line chart by month
- **Y-axis:** Count of out-of-stock incidents (150-220)
- **Pattern:**
  - Jan: ~180 incidents
  - Fluctuates throughout year
  - Dec: ~210 incidents (spike)
- **Trend:** Generally upward toward year-end
- **Insight:** Inventory management challenges; seasonal pressure evident
- **Action:** When spikes occur, investigate supplier delays or demand surges

### Chart 2: Shrinkage % by Department (Horizontal Bar Chart)
- **Type:** Ranked horizontal bar chart
- **Departments (top to bottom):**
  - Food: ~3.0% (highest - perishable goods)
  - Health: ~1.5%
  - Household: ~1.0%
  - Electronics: ~0.5% (lowest)
  - Non-Food: ~0.7%
- **Benchmark:** Retail average is 1.5-2.0%; you're below in most categories
- **Insight:** Food has higher shrinkage (perishable, expiration); Electronics well-controlled
- **Action:** Implement better cold chain for food; maintain current controls for Electronics

### Chart 3: Out of Stock Alert List (Data Table)
- **Type:** Detailed alert table
- **Columns:** Product name, Store location, Date
- **Sample Alerts:**
  - Cadbury Nigeria Dry Goods & Grains Seed
  - Dangote Fresh Produce Perfendis
  - Nestle Bakery Atque
  - P&G Meat & Fish Ipsam
  - And 10+ more items
- **All listed as PrimeChoice locations
- **Alert Duration:** Max 12 hours (current stockouts resolved quickly)
- **Insight:** Current OOS incidents are short-lived; good response time
- **Action:** Monitor these specific items; may indicate seasonal demand or supplier issues

### Chart 4: Inventory Days of Cover by Store (Horizontal Bar Chart)
- **Type:** Ranked bar chart
- **Shows:** Individual store inventory health
- **Visible Stores:**
  - Several stores at 25 days (healthy)
  - Some at 16-15 days (tighter)
- **Range:** 15-25 days across network
- **Color Coding:** Blue bars indicating range
- **Target:** 20-30 days (you're mostly below target)
- **Insight:** Network operating lean; low margin for error
- **Action:** Increase safety stock for top-selling items; improve demand forecasting

### Chart 5: Inventory Value by Department (Pie Chart)
- **Type:** Pie chart with value labels
- **Shows:** ₦ amount tied up in each category
- **Breakdown:**
  - Electronics: Largest segment (dark blue)
  - Clothing: Second largest (light blue)
  - Food: ~₦13bn+ (green)
  - Household: ~₦7bn (lighter)
  - Non-Food: Smaller segment
- **Insight:** Electronics represents largest working capital investment
- **Action:** Focus inventory optimization on Electronics; high ROI opportunity

### Slicers
- **Region:** View regional store performance
- **Store:** Drill into individual store inventory
- **Year:** Track inventory health over time

### Key Questions This Page Answers
1. Are we running out of products?
2. Which products/stores are problematic?
3. How much waste do we have?
4. Is our inventory level sustainable?

## Page 4: Supply Chain & Logistics

**Audience:** Supply Chain Lead, Procurement Manager, Logistics Team  
**Refresh Frequency:** Daily  
**Use Case:** Supplier performance, cost control, delivery reliability

### Purpose
Supplier and logistics performance tracking. Critical for ensuring product availability and controlling procurement costs.

### KPI Cards (Top Row)

| Metric | Value | Insight |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **OTIF %** | 21% | On-Time-In-Full performance (TARGET: >90%) 🔴 CRITICAL ALERT |
| **Avg Lead Time** | 6.05 days | Average delivery time from supplier (acceptable range: 5-10 days) ✅ |
| **Fill Rate %** | 93.67% | % of ordered quantity received (good: >95% target) ✅ |

🔴 **CRITICAL ALERT:** OTIF of 21% is severely below target. Top 5 suppliers all scoring 63-67% (vs 90% target). Indicates systemic supply chain failure requiring immediate intervention.

### Chart 1: Supplier OTIF Scorecard (Horizontal Bar Chart)
- **Type:** Ranked bar chart (Top 5 suppliers visible)
- **Shows:** OTIF % per supplier
- **Top Suppliers (All Below Target):**
  - Supplier 1: 67% (below 90% target)
  - Supplier 2: 66% (below 90% target)
  - Supplier 3: 64% (below 90% target)
  - Supplier 4: 64% (below 90% target)
  - Supplier 5: 63% (below 90% target)
- **Reference Line:** 90% target visible
- **Insight:** ALL suppliers significantly underperforming (63-67% vs 90% target); explains 21% network OTIF
- **Action:** 🔴 CRITICAL - Immediate escalation required; all suppliers need urgent SLA renegotiation or replacement

### Chart 2: Lead Time Variance Analysis (Scatter Plot)
- **Type:** Scatter chart
- **X-axis:** Lead Time Days (0-15 days)
- **Y-axis:** Lead Time Variance (actual - expected, in days)
- **Bubble Colors:** Supplier type legend (3PL, DirectFarm, Distributor, Manufacturer)
- **Pattern:** Mix of early (negative variance) and late (positive variance) suppliers
- **Insight:** Supplier consistency varies; some reliably early, others regularly late
- **Action:** Adjust safety stock based on supplier predictability; penalize consistently late suppliers

### Chart 3: Procurement Cost Trend (Stacked Area Chart)
- **Type:** Stacked area chart
- **X-axis:** Months (Jan-Dec)
- **Segments:** Regular suppliers (light blue) + Strategic suppliers (dark blue)
- **Trend:** Costs rising toward December (seasonal demand spike)
- **Range:** ₦0.5bn-₦1.1bn monthly
- **Year-end Peak:** December shows highest procurement costs
- **Insight:** Cost seasonality evident; year-end buildup for holiday season
- **Action:** Pre-negotiate Q4 pricing; lock in contracts for high-volume season

### Chart 4: PO Delivery Performance (Pie Chart)
- **Type:** Pie chart
- **Shows:** Status of purchase orders
- **Breakdown:**
  - On-Time: 4,882 POs (85.94%) - Blue segment
  - Late: 799 POs (14.06%) - Red segment
- **Insight:** 85% on-time delivery rate is reasonable but 14% late is concerning
- **Action:** Investigate root causes of late deliveries; implement carrier performance management

### Chart 5: Supplier Concentration (Pareto/Combo Chart)
- **Type:** Combo chart (bars + cumulative line)
- **X-axis:** Top 8 suppliers by spend
- **Bars:** Individual supplier spend (₦M)
- **Line:** Cumulative % of total spend (trending upward to 100%)
- **80% Threshold:** Shows concentration point
- **Top 4 Suppliers:** Account for majority of spend (concentration risk ⚠️)
- **Example Values:** ₦394M, ₦353M, ₦325M, ₦308M (top 4)
- **Insight:** Heavy reliance on few suppliers = supply chain risk
- **Action:** Develop backup suppliers; negotiate volume discounts; consider supplier consolidation

### Slicers
- **Supplier:** Filter to specific supplier performance
- **Region:** View supplier performance by region served
- **Year:** Track supplier reliability over time

### Key Questions This Page Answers
1. Can suppliers deliver reliably?
2. Which suppliers are problems?
3. How concentrated is our supply base?
4. Are we paying fair prices?

## Page 5: Finance & P&L Bridge

**Audience:** Finance Director, CFO, Finance Analysts, Board  
**Refresh Frequency:** Monthly (after month-close)  
**Use Case:** Budget variance analysis, profitability tracking, financial reporting

### Purpose
Monthly profit & loss analysis with budget comparison. Critical for financial control and board reporting.

### KPI Cards (Top Row)

| Metric | Value | Insight |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Total Revenue** | ₦29.42bn | Cumulative 3-year revenue |
| **Gross Profit** | ₦8.01bn | Revenue minus COGS (shown in green = positive) |
| **Revenue Variance** | -₦276.70bn | Actual vs Budget (🔴 CRITICAL MISS - massive negative) |

🔴 **ALERT:** Revenue variance of -₦276.70bn is enormous. Budget appears severely misaligned with actual performance. Requires urgent investigation.

### Chart 1: Monthly P&L Bridge (Waterfall Chart)
- **Type:** Waterfall visualization
- **Stages:** Revenue → Gross Profit → EBITDA → Opex → COGS → Total
- **Shows:** How profit flows from top-line to bottom-line
- **Color Coding:**
  - Green: Positive contributions (Revenue, Gross Profit increases)
  - Blue: Negative impacts (COGS, Opex reductions)
  - Dark blue: Total (EBITDA)
- **Pattern:** Clear path from ₦29bn revenue → ₦8bn gross profit
- **Insight:** COGS and Opex are biggest drains on profitability
- **Action:** Focus improvement efforts on cost reduction and operational efficiency

### Chart 2: Revenue Actual vs Budget (Combo Chart)
- **Type:** Combo chart (columns + line)
- **Columns:** Actual revenue (blue) by month
- **Line:** Budget revenue (red) target line
- **X-axis:** Months (Jan-Jul shown, more below)
- **Pattern:** Budget line consistently above actual columns
- **Gap:** Red variance bars showing consistent underperformance
- **Insight:** Every month shows actual below budget; budget targets unrealistic
- **Action:** Review forecast methodology; adjust budget baselines; identify sales shortfall root causes

### Chart 3: Monthly P&L Summary Table (Data Table)
- **Type:** Detailed table with conditional formatting
- **Columns:** Month, Year Label, Total Revenue, COGS, Gross Profit
- **Formatting:**

- **Type:** Grouped bar chart
- **X-axis:** Months (Jan-Dec)
- **Bars:** 2022 (light blue), 2023 (medium blue), 2024 (dark blue)
- **Pattern:**

### Chart 5: Profitability Margins Trend (Multi-Line Chart)
- **Type:** Line chart with 3 lines
- **Y-axis:** Percentage (0-30%)
- **Lines:**
  - Gross Margin %: ~27% (dark blue) - stable, healthy
  - Opex %: ~18% (lighter blue) - operating cost ratio
  - EBITDA %: ~22% - bottom-line profitability
- **Pattern:** All margins relatively stable throughout year
- **Insight:** Consistent profitability; margins not compressing/expanding significantly
